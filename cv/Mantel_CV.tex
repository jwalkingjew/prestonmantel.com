\section{Working Papers}
Mantel, Preston. ``Taker Delays and Liquidity Provision in Prediction Markets.'' Working Paper, May 2026.
\begin{itemize}
  \item Studies Polymarket policy changes introducing and removing taker delays in crypto markets.
  \item Uses BBO quotes and on-chain fills to estimate effects on spreads, liquidity, and maker entry.
  \item Finds that taker delays narrow quoted spreads, increase maker entry, and interact with rebates.
\end{itemize}

Mantel, Preston. ``When Lots Matter: SEC Round Lot Modernization and Liquidity.'' Working Paper, November 2025. Available at SSRN: \url{https://ssrn.com/abstract=5757242}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Examines market quality and trading behavior effects of SEC round lot modernization.
  \item Studies how round lot definitions affect trading costs by impacting NBBO spreads.
  \item Finds significant decreases in spreads, volatility, and latency following round lot modernization.
\end{itemize}

Mantel, Preston and Mehmet Saglam. ``Democratizing or Demoralizing: Impact of Robinhood on Costs and Volatility.'' Working Paper, November 2025. Available at SSRN: \url{https://ssrn.com/abstract=4377192}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Studies how Robinhood's order-collaring mechanism affects retail trading costs and volatility.
  \item Uses regular hours and after-hours trades to identify the impact of market order collaring.
  \item Finds increased retail trading costs of approximately 2 basis points, or over \$2 billion in 2020.
\end{itemize}

Mantel, Preston and Mehmet Saglam. ``Market Making Counterparty Interest.'' Working Paper, June 2025.
\begin{itemize}
  \item Tests whether market makers treat institutional counterparties differently from retail flow.
  \item Maps proprietary institutional trades to TAQ NBBO spreads and intraday price impact.
  \item Replicates prior evidence and finds similar treatment across institutional counterparties.
\end{itemize}

Mantel, Preston, Mehmet Saglam, and Tugkan Tuzun (Federal Reserve). ``Actively Traded ETFs.'' Working Paper, June 2025.
\begin{itemize}
  \item Examines conflicts between managers and investors using trades from actively traded ETFs.
  \item Shows ETF trades predict abnormal volume and associates buy trades with positive one-day returns.
  \item Uses short-sale activity to study whether short sellers respond to ETF-driven mispricing.
\end{itemize}
