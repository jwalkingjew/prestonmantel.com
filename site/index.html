Research

Interests: Market Microstructure, Retail Investor Protection, Regulation, Price Discovery, Market Quality

Job Market Paper

When Lots Matter: SEC Round Lot Modernization and Liquidity

  • Examines market quality and trading behavior effects of SEC round lot modernization.
  • Studies how round lot definitions affect trading costs by impacting NBBO spreads.
  • Finds significant decreases in spreads, volatility, and latency following round lot modernization.

Under Review

Democratizing or Demoralizing: Impact of Robinhood on Costs and Volatility

with Mehmet Sağlam

Semi-finalist, Best Paper in Market Microstructure, FMA 2023Media: Bloomberg

  • Studies how Robinhood's order-collaring mechanism affects retail trading costs and volatility.
  • Uses regular hours and after-hours trades to identify the impact of market order collaring.
  • Finds increased retail trading costs of approximately 2 basis points, or over $2 billion in 2020.

Presented at: Financial Management Association Annual Meeting, Chicago (2023); The Microstructure Exchange, Stockholm (2023); Southern Finance Association Annual Meeting (2024)

Working Papers

Taker Delays and Liquidity Provision in Prediction Markets

  • Studies Polymarket policy changes introducing and removing taker delays in crypto markets.
  • Uses BBO quotes and on-chain fills to estimate effects on spreads, liquidity, and maker entry.
  • Finds that taker delays narrow quoted spreads, increase maker entry, and interact with rebates.

Draft available upon request.

Market Making Counterparty Interest

with Mehmet Sağlam

  • Tests whether market makers treat institutional counterparties differently from retail flow.
  • Maps proprietary institutional trades to TAQ NBBO spreads and intraday price impact.
  • Replicates prior evidence and finds similar treatment across institutional counterparties.

Draft available upon request.

Actively Traded ETFs

with Mehmet Sağlam and Tugkan Tuzun (Federal Reserve Board)

  • Examines conflicts between managers and investors using trades from actively traded ETFs.
  • Shows ETF trades predict abnormal volume and associates buy trades with positive one-day returns.
  • Uses short-sale activity to study whether short sellers respond to ETF-driven mispricing.

Draft available upon request.
